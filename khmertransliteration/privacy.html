Privacy Policy

Khmer Transliteration · Last updated: April 8, 2026

Overview

Khmer Transliteration is a keyboard app that converts Latin romanization into Khmer script. This policy explains what data is collected and how it is used.

What We Collect

When you submit a new word using the "Add Word" feature, the Latin-to-Khmer word pair is sent to our server. This data is used to improve transliteration accuracy for all users by helping train and refine our transliteration model.

Submitted word pairs contain only the Latin romanization and the Khmer text you entered. We do not collect:

  • Keystrokes or any text you type in other apps
  • Personal information (name, email, location, etc.)
  • Device identifiers or IP addresses for tracking purposes
  • Any data from the keyboard outside of explicit word submissions

How We Use the Data

Word submissions are used solely to improve the Khmer transliteration model. They may be reviewed, aggregated, and incorporated into training data. Submissions are not sold, shared with third parties, or used for advertising.

Keyboard Privacy

The keyboard extension does not request Full Access. It cannot access the internet for any purpose other than submitting words you explicitly add. Normal typing through the keyboard is processed entirely on your device and is never recorded or transmitted.

Local Storage

Submitted word pairs are also stored locally on your device to provide immediate personalized suggestions. This data remains on your device and can be cleared by removing the app.

Third-Party Services

No other third-party services are used.

Children's Privacy

Khmer Transliteration does not knowingly collect any data from children under the age of 13.

Changes to This Policy

If we update this privacy policy, the revised version will be posted here with an updated date. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
